About Us - The Technology Conglomerate Stark Industries

Stark industries is a multinational tech and industry conglomerate with 390 facilities, 340 in the United States and 50 of them internationally.
We manufacture radar systems, aircraft engines, AIS Transponders, batteries, LIDAR-sensors, arc reactors, head-up-display helmets, robots, and exoskeleton suits. We have 430,000 employees worldwide who, everyday, work on the mission to advance the boundaries of technology and helping our clients take on the most challenging and importantions.

We strive to remain one step ahead by designing and developing the most technically sophisticated products in the world. Turning Sci-fi into reality is what we do.

Our history and ambitions

Stark Industries is a defense technology company that was founded by Howard Stark in 1940.
Already in the beginning of the history of our company, we helped the United States Armed Forces by providing innovative weapons. Howard's ambitions to accelerate humanity's leap towards a better future culminated in one of his inventions, the Arc Reactor.
During the later years of his life, sometime after his involvement in World War II, Howard Stark developed
the idea for an alternative power source that could provide clean energy to benefit his humanitarian views for the future of mankind.

Stark saw the Arc Reactor as the stepping stone to create a new, stable element that would render nuclear power obsolete. However, Vanko saw the Arc Reactor as a way to get rich and when Stark found out, he had Vanko deported to the Soviet Union.
Howard began working alongside Anton Vanko during the 1960s and together they eventually designed the first Arc Reactor, which Howard began to develop through his company, Stark Industries.

In the years after Howard's death, the Arc Reactor project became a non-priority to the company as it was steered towards mainly producing weapons, with the then acting CEO, Obadiah Stane, dismissing the clean energy project as a "publicity stunt".
A large Arc Reactor industrial energy production was created to power Stark Industries' previous headquarters in Los Angeles.



Milestones

In 1974, Stark designed the Stark Expo, the "City of the Future," but was unable to finish it due to limited technology.
Howard Stark died in 1991, leaving ownership of the company to his son Tony and placed his partner Stane as CEO.

In 1991 Stark Industries won the Arthur M Wellington prize (American Society of Civil Engineers)

Stark Industries has won a CES Innovations Award 5 times (2006, 2007, 2009, 2013, 2019)

In 2007 Stark Industries acquired Fujikawa and AccuTech. Fujikawa is a japanese company specialised in researching holography and heads-up displays for various uses. AccuTech, specialised in weapons and security systems, cooperated with Stark Industries to develop weapons such as the Jericho missile and the Stark Sonic Cannon.

In 2009 Former CEO Tony Stark conceived a groundbreaking, cutting-edge invention that would offer clean energy efficiently to the whole world; The Arc Reactor. Although his father Howard Stark had invented an Arc Reactor decades before, Tony Stark's Arc Reactor was astonishing because of its size. In the beginning, the public was not informed about the invention. Eventually, Tony Stark decided to continue his father's ideals to use the Arc Reactor to provide clean energy for the world. In order to profit on this ideal, Tony Stark created a redesigned Industrial Arc Reactor to power the newly-built Stark Tower in Manhattan, New York. The building was completely removed from the city's power grid and relied entirely on the Arc Reactor in order to make Stark Tower a “beacon of clean energy” and show the world the capability of Arc Reactor technology on a large scale. Even though the Arc Reactor was only a prototype, the building was capable of maintaining itself for an entire year.

In 2014 the Collier Trophy, an annual aviation award administered by the U.S. National Aeronautic Association (NAA), was awarded to Stark Industries for its research in Ramjet propulsion. The Collier Trophy is the most prestigious aviation award, annually given for the greatest achievement in aeronautics or astronautics.

In 2015 an employee at Stark Industries won the NAS Award in Aeronautical Engineering, which is an award awarded by the U.S. National Academy of Sciences "for excellence in the field of aeronautical engineering."

In 2010, Stark Industries was the world's 3rd largest weapons manufacturer Had Tony Stark decided to continue the weapons manufacturing division, we would have remained at the same rank.

Our weapons have been showcased on the annually held Stark Expo, and they have been on active deployment in battlefields in Afghanistan and Iraq. While we no longer produce or sell weapons, the weapons we have created have displayed combat-capability that has been much appreciated by our clients.

The Future of Stark Industries

"I came to realize that I have more to offer this world than just making things that blow up. And that is why, effective immediately, I am shutting down the weapons manufacturing division of Stark Industries until such a time as I can decide what the future of the company will be." ―Tony Stark

Maintaining the moral compass is a crucial part of our mission. Unlike many weapon conglomerates, we have chosen a different path; we don't participate in grim conflicts worldwide.
Anthony "Tony" Stark witnessed that the potential of the company should averted from that direction and that the target of Stark Industries should instead be to become more focused on improving people's lives and the world by pushing the boundaries of today's technology.
